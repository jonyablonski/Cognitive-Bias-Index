---
title: "Pre-Mortem Analysis"
description: "A structured exercise where you assume a decision or project has already failed and work backward to identify why. It exposes hidden risks driven by optimism bias and groupthink."
categories: ["decision making"]
tags: ["Planning", "Group"]
how_to_use:
  - "Clearly define the decision or plan."
  - "Assume it failed 6–12 months from now."
  - "Ask: 'What went wrong?'"
  - "Have individuals write answers independently."
  - "Group and synthesize failure themes."
  - "Identify preventable risks."
  - "Turn top risks into actions and owners."
when_to_use: "Before major decisions, launches, or commitments."
tip: "Do silent idea generation first—this avoids anchoring on the loudest voice"
use_cases: "Product launches, roadmap bets, hiring decisions, partnerships"
---
